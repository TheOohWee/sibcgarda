Earnings Call Analysis 

Analyze earnings calls from a diverse group of companies to understand corporate outlook on various macro economic signals from distinct corners of the market, allowing for the constructing a bottom-up view of the economy. 

Macro Themes 

Data Sourcing & Infrastructure 

Consumer & Retail 
The closest sector to average Americans; by capturing the everyday spending habits of citizens, C & R firms often see the immediate effects of inflation and unemployment on consumption habits

Transportation & Logistics
The backbone of all physical industries; the costs or struggles that these firms face will trickle into the statements of most other businesses, making their reactions to inflation or unemployment especially potent  

Heavy Machinery
A traditional center for CapEx spending and physical buildout; the success of these firms often reflects the growth or decay of other divisions of the market and (recently with AI) can be an important marker in understanding the progress of new technology adoption  

Macro Themes Today in Earnings Calls

Technology 
A burgeoning sector synonymous with advancement and change; access to capital and headcount numbers in this space and reflect the coming or slowing momentum of the economy 

In today’s markets we can see the importance of viewing the economy from a bottom-up conception. In particular, tariffs and unemployment provide potent examples. Tariffs were largely misunderstood and better described by viewing individual corporate experiences with them, such as General Mills or Pepsi. While, unemployment numbers have seen great variance, with UPS, MS, and Amazon all acting as examples of numbers diverging from once promising revisions.
